Compensation, Benefits, and Career Outlook in New Mexico

Salary Expectations in New Mexico

Compensation is a key consideration. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) data for May 2023, the mean hourly wage for Aircraft Mechanics and Service Technicians (SOC code 49-3011) in New Mexico was $34.68, with a mean annual wage of $72,130 [bls_oes_nm_493011]. The estimated employment for this occupation within the state was 870 individuals [bls_oes_nm_493011].

It's important to note that the New Mexico mean annual wage was slightly lower than the U.S. national mean of $76,950 [bls_oes_nm_493011]. Entry-level wages are typically lower, increasing with experience and certifications.

Benefits Packages

Beyond direct wages, comprehensive benefits packages are standard in the industry and contribute significantly to total compensation. Typical benefits include health insurance (medical, dental, vision), retirement savings plans (such as 401(k) plans, often with employer matching contributions), paid time off (vacation, sick leave, holidays), and life or disability insurance. Technicians employed directly by airlines may also receive valuable flight benefits for themselves and eligible family members. Specific offerings vary by employer. For instance, a major aerospace employer like The Aerospace Corporation highlights its provision of comprehensive benefits packages that include health coverage, retirement plans, paid time off, and support for professional development [aec_careers_benefits], illustrating the types of packages available at prominent New Mexico employers in this sector.

Job Market Demand and Employment Outlook in NM

The outlook is robust, supported by state-level factors and national trends. New Mexico's focus on Aerospace and Defense indicates ongoing support [nm_dws_aerospace].

The national shortage of A&P mechanics creates high demand [aviation_pros_shortage]. Projections suggest a need for thousands of new mechanics nationwide. This translates to strong job security for qualified technicians in New Mexico. The combination of state focus and national shortage means graduates from programs like CNM and ENMU-Roswell [cnm_amt_program, enmu_roswell_amt] are entering a favorable market.
